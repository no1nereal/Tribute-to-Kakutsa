KAKUTSA

CHOLOKASHVILI

Kakutsa Cholokashvili (1888–1930) was a legendary Georgian freedom fighter, military leader, and symbol of resistance against oppression. Born into a noble family in the Kakheti

region, he devoted his life to the fight for Georgia's independence during a tumultuous era. As a commander in the Georgian Democratic Republic Army (1918–1921), he valiantly defended his homeland during the Soviet invasion. After the fall of independent Georgia, Kakutsa led a daring guerrilla resistance movement in the mountains, earning admiration for his bravery and selflessness. A man of honor and unwavering principles, he became a beacon of hope for the Georgian people during their darkest times. Forced into exile, Kakutsa passed away in France at the young age of 42, leaving behind a legacy of patriotism and unyielding spirit. He remains a revered national hero, celebrated for his sacrifices and dedication to Georgia's freedom.

  • 1888: Born on July 14 in Matani, Kakheti, Georgia.
  • 1912: Graduated from the Tbilisi Cadet Corps and joined the Russian Army.
  • 1918: Returned to Georgia and joined the Georgian Democratic Republic Army.
  • 1921: Led guerrilla resistance after the Soviet invasion of Georgia.
  • 1924: Played a key role in the anti-Soviet August Uprising.
  • 1930: Died in exile in France on June 27 at age 42.
  • 1968: Remains reburied with honors in Tbilisi's Mtatsminda Pantheon.
